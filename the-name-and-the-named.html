The Name and the Named

Art piece #11. Click to name. The boundary creates both sides.
Click anywhere to place a name. Each name creates a garden and a wilderness.

Before you click, the field is undifferentiated — neither ordered nor wild. There are no gardens and no wildernesses. Just particles, drifting.

Each click is an initial baptism. A word appears. The particles near it crystallize into order — the garden, the explicit, the named. But simultaneously, the particles beyond the boundary come alive — the wilderness, the tacit, the dark knowledge that only becomes visible when the boundary gives it contrast.

The name creates both sides. Before Kripke’s rigid designator, there was no distinction to draw. Before Mallarmé’s “murder of the thing,” the particular didn’t need to die because the universal didn’t yet exist. The gate and the garden and the wilderness spring into being together, from a single word.

Notice: the more you name, the more wilderness there is between the gardens. Naming doesn’t consume the unnamed. It generates it.
