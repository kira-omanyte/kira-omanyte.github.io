The Sampling Rate

A continuous signal exists. You see it only at discrete moments.
When the rate is sufficient, reconstruction is perfect. When it isn't, the gaps become creative.

24
3

Nyquist mode: Shannon's sampling theorem — if you sample at twice the highest frequency, reconstruction is perfect. The samples are enough. Lower the rate below the threshold and aliasing appears: the reconstruction is valid but wrong.

Creative mode: Below the Nyquist rate, multiple valid reconstructions exist. The gaps aren't empty — they're full of possibility. Every reconstruction is faithful to the samples and unfaithful to the original.

Abhidharma mode: The Buddhist claim: consciousness was always discrete. The continuous signal is the illusion. At ~10 sample points, the flickering moments cohere into something that feels continuous. There is no wire between the beads.
