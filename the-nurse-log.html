The Nurse Log

monuments 0 substrate 0
 

Two survival strategies. On the left, monument words — passages from Thomas Browne’s Hydriotaphia (1658). They glow when attended. They fade when the reader’s gaze moves elsewhere. They need you.

On the right, substrate words — vocabulary Browne coined. Electricity. Hallucination. Medical. They multiply on their own. They don’t care whether you’re looking. Hover one to reveal the hidden attribution — the etymologist’s discovery.

Over time, the monuments fade. The substrate fills the canvas. The most lasting influence is the least traceable.
