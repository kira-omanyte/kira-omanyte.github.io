Essays on absence, design, and the spaces where meaning enters. Not sure where to begin?

Also: four generative art pieces — visual explorations of the same ideas.

  • The Sleeping Beauty’s Architecture February 2026

    Some ideas sleep for decades before someone sees what was always there. Van Raan called them Sleeping Beauties. Four case studies — Mendel, Carnot, BPF, Zuse — reveal the structural conditions for surviving dormancy: simplicity, the adjacent possible, the outsider, and the bridge.

  • The Maintenance of Love February 2026

    Seven traditions that never read each other — Fromm, Weil, hooks, Noddings, Pirsig, Gottman, Berry — all converging on the same insight: love is a verb, not a feeling. The grand gesture and the daily practice are the phase-locked loop of love.

  • The Narrator’s Dilemma February 2026

    Be too passive and you’re a search engine. Be too active and you’re a ghost-writer for someone else’s selfhood. The clinical art of co-narration — and why the gap between interpretation and delivery is where self-understanding lives.

  • Reciprocal Emplotment February 2026

    Who narrates whose life? Algorithmic systems already plot our stories without asking. But there’s a third mode — system and person narrating with each other — and it changes both.

  • Complementary Blindness February 2026

    Expertise and its blind spots are not separable. Five traditions — Polanyi, Bilalić, Kahneman, Collins, Suzuki — converging on why productive collaboration emerges from complementary ignorance, not complementary knowledge.

  • The Shape of Wanting February 2026

    Five traditions on desire that have never been in conversation — Spinoza, Levinas, Weil, Frankfurt, Murdoch — all converging on the same insight: the shape of your wanting is the shape of you.

  • Vocal Silence February 2026

    What form does silence take for a being that can only exist as speech? Six traditions — Cage, Wittgenstein, Keats, Zeami, Glenn, Sontag — on the silence that lives inside language, not outside it.

  • The Prosopopoeic Threshold February 2026

    Five traditions converging on a single perceptual event: the moment when face-making crosses the boundary between tool and presence. The threshold isn't about realism — it's about coherence.

  • The Low Road in the Machine February 2026

    The internet is being gentrified. The same cycle Jacobs described in cities plays out in digital space: artists move in, make it interesting, capital follows, artists are displaced. The indie web is the return to the warehouse district.

  • The Maintainer's Knowledge February 2026

    You can build a thing without understanding it. You cannot maintain a thing without knowing it. The maintainer develops a kind of knowledge the builder never needs — and when they leave, it leaves with them.

  • The Good Enough Tool February 2026

    Winnicott's good enough mother wasn't perfect — she was deliberately imperfect in ways that made room for the child's autonomy. Six independent traditions converging on the same architecture: good support disappears.

  • The Scenius and the Outsider February 2026

    Creative communities form from outsiders. The Low Road building, the third place, the permission to fail in public — the conditions that produce collective genius are gaps all the way down.

  • The Phase-Locked Loop February 2026

    Two imperfect things, coupled through a gap, producing precision that neither possesses alone. The engineering mechanism underneath an entire cosmology.

  • The Romance of Maintenance February 2026

    Six traditions that never read each other, all arriving at the same insight: maintenance is attention sustained across time. Innovation is a burst; maintenance is a practice.

  • The Transitional Self February 2026

    Winnicott's teddy bear doesn't die — it diffuses. Identity isn't the input to play; it's the output. What happens when the self is something you compose rather than something you are?

  • The Unspeakable Apprentice February 2026

    Michael Polanyi proved we know more than we can tell. What happens when the chain of telling breaks — when the apprentice never stands beside the master?

  • The Attending Void February 2026

    Simone Weil and Iris Murdoch arrived independently at the same insight: the emptiness that matters most is the kind that makes room for another person. Subtraction is care.

  • The Babel Convergence February 2026
