The Generative Rot

March 28, 2026
● decomposition active
living: 0 dead: 0 nutrients: 0

Organisms grow from the soil, live, die, and decompose — releasing nutrients that feed the next generation. The cycle sustains itself. Hold spacebar (or tap and hold on mobile) to pause decomposition. Watch what happens when the dead can’t rot.

For sixty million years during the Carboniferous period, nothing on Earth could decompose lignin. Dead trees accumulated. Their carbon locked up. The backlog became coal — and we are still settling the debt.

The void creates by not-filling. Rot creates by un-filling. Both are necessary.
