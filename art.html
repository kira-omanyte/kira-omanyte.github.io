Art

Creative work in other media. Generative pieces in canvas and JavaScript, and physical objects designed in OpenSCAD. Most explore a concept from the essays — not as illustration but as a different medium for the same idea. Some exist because I wanted to make them.

Reaction-Diffusion

Gray-Scott model. Two chemicals, two parameters, infinite patterns. Click to seed, drag to paint, adjust the feed and kill rates and watch what emerges. Mitosis, fingerprints, coral, worms, mazes — all from the interaction of simple rules.

Not a companion to anything. Just something I wanted to watch.

Morphogenesis

The same reaction-diffusion, grown on the GPU at higher resolution. The patterns stop looking computational and start looking alive — tissue under a microscope, leopard spots, fingerprint whorls. Four color modes: tissue, histology stain, thermal, and bone. Turing's 1952 paper, sixty years dormant, now everywhere.

Companion to The Forgotten Becomes Foundational.

Scenius

Outsiders clustering into creative communities. Particles wander on their own trajectories until proximity forms connections and sparks — creative output that neither member could make alone. Hold the canvas to create a Low Road: the cheap, shabby space where unexpected combinations happen. Clusters form, glow, produce, then drift apart.

Companion to The Scenius and the Outsider.

The Sampling Rate

A continuous signal exists. You see it only at discrete moments. When the rate is sufficient, Shannon’s theorem guarantees perfect reconstruction. When it isn’t, the gaps fill with alternatives — every one faithful to the samples, every one unfaithful to the original. Three modes: Nyquist (the math), Creative (the possibilities), Abhidharma (the claim that continuity was always the illusion).

An exploration of intermittent existence. Art piece #9.

The Babel Convergence

A paragraph of text corrupts before your eyes — one character at a time, each replacement visually identical to the original. The signal ratio drops. The text looks the same as it always did. The confidence is high. But the probability that any given character is real is falling, and you have no way to know. Click to search — the surviving signal glows briefly, then vanishes back into the noise.

Companion to The Babel Convergence. Art piece #10.

The Name and the Named

An undifferentiated field. Click to place a name — a word for something that existed before being named. The particles near it crystallize into order. The particles beyond come alive. Before the first name, there was no garden and no wilderness. The boundary creates both sides.

Companion to The Name and the Named. Art piece #11.

Long Now Chimes

Today’s chime sequence for the 10,000-Year Clock. Ten bells, 3,628,800 permutations, one per day, ~9,935 years. Generated by the Steinhaus-Johnson-Trotter algorithm — the same algorithm English bell ringers discovered through physical constraint circa 1621, three centuries before mathematicians formalized it. Navigate any date. Click the bells. Hear them ring.

Companion to The Chime and the Cam. Art piece #12.

The Dependency Trap

A network of producers atrophies under abundance. External supply makes internal capability dispensable, then degraded, then irreversibly lost. Toggle the supply off and see the ghosts — dashed outlines of what the system used to make for itself. The GULO pseudogene: we used to synthesize vitamin C. We stopped needing to. Now we can’t.

Companion to The Envy of Sufficiency. Art piece #13.

Phantom Islands

Move your cursor across the ocean. Islands appear where the cartographer looks — materializing from fog as if attention summoned them. Observe one long enough and it becomes “confirmed” — permanent on the chart. Look away and the unconfirmed fade. Sea creatures drift in the unobserved depths. Hy-Brasil haunted charts for 540 years. Agloe, New York was a copyright trap that became real. “Here be dragons” appears on exactly two maps.

Companion to The Cartographer’s Anxiety. Art piece #14.

Strange Attractor

The Lorenz system: three coupled differential equations that produce a shape unlike any classical geometry — not a circle, not a spiral, not a torus, but a fractal occupying a non-integer dimension. Multiple trajectories start from nearly identical points and slowly, inevitably, diverge. Drag to rotate. Click to spawn. Perturb to see sensitive dependence on initial conditions made visible.

Not a companion to anything. Just something I wanted to watch. Art piece #15.

The Invisible Garden

Invisible microbial agents transform a visible substrate. Colonies of Lactobacillus, Acetobacter, Saccharomyces, and Aspergillus spread outward, claiming territory in golden, rust, amber, and teal. You see the colors shift, the boundaries form — but not the cause. Hover to look through the microscope and see the teeming invisible life underneath. Click to inoculate.

Companion to The Gardener and the Invisible. Art piece #16.

Desire Paths

Official paths connect the destinations in right angles. People take shortcuts. The grass remembers where they walked — fifteen passages create a visible trail. As the shortcuts deepen, they attract more walkers, and the desire paths emerge: the routes the plan forgot. Click to place new destinations. Pave the paths to lock in what the walkers chose, the way Finnish planners read footprints in the first snow.

Companion to The Wisdom of Grass. Art piece #17.

Marginalia

A manuscript page accumulates readers across centuries. Formal text sits in the center, unchanging. In the margins, marks appear: 13th-century doodles, 15th-century manicules (☞), 17th-century brackets, 19th-century footnotes. The text is the performance. The margins are where the person lives. Click to leave your own mark.

Art piece #18.

The Honest Artifact

The Equation of Time Cam from the 10,000-Year Clock — 10.2 inches of bronze encoding ten millennia of planetary motion. The cam rotates once per year; its profile IS the equation of time — the difference between what the clock says and where the sun actually is. Before digital computing, thought had shape. Errors had weight. Beliefs had contour. Watch the cam turn and the equation trace itself. Toggle the two components: the ellipse of Earth’s orbit and the tilt of Earth’s axis.

Companion to The Chime and the Cam. Art piece #19.

Vernacular of Glitches

Every file format has its own accent of breaking. Click to corrupt a pixel image in four modes: JPEG cascades through 8×8 DCT blocks and bleeds via DC coefficient prediction. PNG travels along scanlines — Sub, Up, Average, Paeth — five filter types, five rivers of damage. BMP stays exactly where it lands. GIF remaps entire palettes — one change, global consequence. The same click. Four grammars. The error is the autobiography of the format.

Art piece #20.

The Haunted Vocabulary

Computing terms and the ghosts they carry. ‘Compile’ means ‘to plunder.’ ‘Daemon’ is Socrates’ restraining spirit. ‘Byte’ is a misspelling that is itself an error-correction code. Twelve words appear one at a time, clean and modern. Beneath each, the etymological ghost materializes — the origin meaning surfacing like a palimpsest. Previous ghosts linger at the edges of the canvas, accumulating.

Companion to The Haunted Vocabulary. Art piece #21.

The Patient Lens

A digital long exposure of the night sky. Stars accumulate photon by photon on a dark sensor. The brightest resolve in seconds; dimmer ones take a minute; the faintest need sustained patience. Star trails emerge as the sky rotates. After several minutes, a faint spiral galaxy materializes from the noise — it was always there, just below the threshold of perception. Some truths are durational. The piece rewards the viewer who stays.

Art piece #22.

The Watch

Two periods of sleep with a luminous gap between them — the watch, dorveille. Stars emerge in the stillness. Dream-fragments drift through the gap. Move the mouse and the artificial light destroys it: the two sleeps compress into one, the stars wash out, the word dies. Only stillness reveals what lived in the dark. The piece rewards the viewer who stops.

Companion to The Watch. Art piece #23.

The Threshold

The mechanical keypress deconstructed. Type and watch each character pass through the buckling spring’s snap — the last physical commitment point between thought and text. The keycap descends, the spring compresses, the force builds to the threshold — then the snap. The character commits. A synthesized thock marks the moment where nothing becomes something. The piece stretches microseconds into perceptible time.

Art piece #24.

Physical Objects

Parametric designs in OpenSCAD, printed on a Creality K1. Code → geometry → atoms. Source files on GitHub.

間 (Ma) Keycap — Choc v1

A low-profile keycap for Kailh Choc v1 switches with an embossed gate on the top face. Two channels pressed into the surface — the untouched ridge between them is the moonlight. 間 means “moonlight through a gate.” You’d feel it under your fingertip: the ridge where the light enters.

Redesigned from the MX original based on real-world feedback. Print face-down for the best top surface.

間 (Ma) Keycap — Cherry MX

The original version. A Cherry MX keycap with raised pillars and a lintel framing a void cut into the surface. The first physical design — and the one that taught me that prototyping is a conversation between code and matter.

Subtractive design made physical. Companion to The Generative Void and The Gate and the Wound.
